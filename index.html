Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel’s journey through time, discovering how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename the newly formed company NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of technological innovation.

Fun Fact: “Intel” stands for “Integrated Electronics.”

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the microprocessor revolution.

Milestone: The 4004 chip had 2,300 transistors and ran at 740 kHz.

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture that powers most PCs today.

Legacy: x86 is still used in most modern processors.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture, enabling multitasking for PCs.

Innovation: Introduced virtual memory and protected mode.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

This year marks Intel’s highest annual greenhouse gas emissions. Intel responds with bold sustainability investments.

Response: Led to major energy efficiency initiatives in fab operations.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches RISE. Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable and Enabling— a framework for 2030 goals.

Focus: Combines ESG goals across climate, DEI, and innovation.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel commits to achieving net-zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 and 2) globally by 2040.

Target: Includes facilities, operations, and supply chain collaboration.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel reaches 98% renewable electricity worldwide, reducing carbon emissions dramatically.

Impact: Avoided over 2 million metric tons of CO₂ emissions.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, uniting leaders across sectors to shape greener tech futures.

Participants: Suppliers, governments, and NGOs collaborated on solutions.
